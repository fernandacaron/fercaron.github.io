research

My Ph.D. research is still being developed and will be available here soon. My master's dissertation aimed to investigate general patterns in the evolution of body size in terrestrial vertebrates. In particular, I've focused on identifying general patterns in the evolution of body size among amphibians, squamates, birds, and mammals, assessing whether evolution follows a model where evolution is constant over time and whether there are differences in the evolution of body size between groups. Moreover, I also assessed how sexual size dimorphism (SSD) evolves in birds, analyzing how character evolution takes place, how its evolution and body size are related, and the ecological and spatial correlates for SSD.
